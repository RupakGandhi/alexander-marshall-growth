## The 60‑second story you can tell your principals

1. Mr. Allard observes Ms. Stahosky's art class for 12 minutes. He types in Scripted Notes — it auto‑saves every keystroke, no "save" button to forget.
2. He clicks a 2 on "Student ownership of learning" and a 3 on "Clarity of objectives." He presses **Generate feedback**. Glows, grows, focus areas, and next steps appear — all editable.
3. He signs once. Ms. Stahosky gets a notification bell ping on her phone.
4. She opens the observation. She sees a blue "read this first" banner, then strengths, growth areas, focus areas, next steps, rubric scores — **then** the signature box. She signs and optionally types a response.
5. Because "Student ownership of learning" was a 2, the platform auto‑enrolls her in a 90‑minute PD module: "Level 2 → 3: redesign your next student‑ownership lesson." She opens it, picks an upcoming Tuesday lesson, checks off which Level‑3 signals are already there, reads the rubric side by side, rewrites the lesson, scripts her opener/pivot/close, picks one student evidence artifact, teaches it, submits the bundle.
6. Mr. Allard gets a review ping. He verifies or asks for one more pass. The module is marked complete. She levels up — on the exact indicator he coded her on.

One eval → one targeted PD → one classroom artifact. Not a whole‑group PD day where the CTE teacher is in an ELA session.
