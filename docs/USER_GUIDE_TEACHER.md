## Welcome

The Marshall Growth Platform is where you'll see every formal and informal observation of your teaching, track your growth areas, and access the resources the district has curated to help you grow on the Marshall rubric.

**A few things to know up front:**
- **Nothing** is shared with you until your principal officially **publishes** an observation. Drafts and work-in-progress are invisible to you.
- Your **private notes from the principal are never shown to you** (those are admin-only working notes). What you see is the official, signed record.
- You can **respond** when you sign an observation — this becomes part of the permanent record alongside the observation itself.
- The platform is **yours** — you can export your full record at any time.

## Signing In

1. Open https://alexander-marshall-growth.pages.dev on any device.

## Your Dashboard

**URL:** `/teacher`

The dashboard is your one-stop view. It shows:

### Active Focus Areas (left card)
A list of the specific growth areas your principal has opened with you, each showing:
- The linked rubric indicator (e.g. *D.2 — Checking for Understanding*)

### Recent Published Observations (right card)
Your 5 most recent published observations with:
- Type (Mini, Formal, Annual Summary)
- Date
- Appraiser name

## My Observations

**URL:** `/teacher/observations`

The complete list of every officially-published observation of your teaching. Columns:
- **Date**
- **Type** — Mini, Formal, Annual Summary
- **Subject / Context** — what you were teaching that day

## Reading an Observation

When you open an observation you'll see, in order:

### Header
- Type (Mini / Formal / Annual Summary)
- Date and time observed
- Appraiser's name and title
- Class context (e.g. *3rd period Algebra I, 24 students*)
- Status badge

### Overall summary
A teacher-facing summary from your principal — usually a paragraph or two. This is the place to start.

### Rubric scores
Every indicator your principal scored. Each score shows:
- **The indicator** (e.g. *A.3 Planning and Preparation — Designing Coherent Instruction*)
- **The level** — 4 Highly Effective / 3 Effective / 2 Improvement Necessary / 1 Does Not Meet
- **The evidence note** your principal wrote — the specific observable behavior that led to this score

Indicators left unscored simply don't appear — it means your principal didn't see enough evidence during this observation.

### Strengths (Glows)
What your principal saw you doing well.

### Growth areas (Grows)
Where your principal thinks you can develop.

### Focus areas
Long-term growth threads — these live across multiple observations until they're achieved. See the [Focus Areas](#focus-areas) section for more.

### Suggested Next Steps
Specific, short-term things to try before the next observation.

### Signatures
- **Your principal's signature** — already there, with the date they signed
- **Your signature** (if you've acknowledged)

**What you WON'T see:**
- Private appraiser notes (admin-only, by design)
- Drafts or earlier versions
- Other teachers' data

## Acknowledging an Observation

Once you've read an observation marked **Published**:

1. Scroll to the bottom — you'll see a **"Sign & Acknowledge"** box.
2. Optionally type a **response** in the text area. This is your chance to:
   - Agree / disagree with any part of the observation
   - Add context the principal might not have known ("I was covering a colleague's absence")
   - Ask a question that will become part of the record
   - Celebrate something specific
   Your response is preserved forever alongside the observation.
3. **Sign** in the signature box — use your mouse or your finger on mobile. Tap **Clear** if you need to redo.
4. Click **Sign & Acknowledge**.

The status flips to **Acknowledged** and your signature is time-stamped into the permanent record.

**Important — acknowledging is NOT agreement.** Signing means "I've read this and had a chance to respond." You can completely disagree with a score and still acknowledge — in fact, that's what the response field is for.

**What if you never sign?** Policy varies by district — check with your association / HR. Technically you can leave an observation forever in "Published" state without acknowledging. The system won't force you.

## Focus Areas

**URL:** `/teacher/focus`

Your active and closed growth threads. Each active focus area shows:

- **The indicator** you're working on (domain + indicator code + full name)
- **The title** your principal wrote (e.g. *"Increase student-to-student discourse"*)
- **A description** of what "success" looks like
- **When it was opened**
- **Teacher next moves** — concrete strategies the Pedagogy Library recommends for your current level
- **Resources** — articles, videos, or books recommended at your current level

**Why focus areas matter:**
- They're your **growth plan** for the year, chosen specifically for you based on your observations
- Every time you open this page, the resources are tailored to the level you're at — so if you move from a 2 to a 3, the suggestions change accordingly
- Your coach (if you have one) is looking at the same page, so your coaching conversations are focused

**When does a focus area close?**
Your principal decides — usually at the next formal observation, if the evidence shows you've grown. You can't close them yourself.

## Your Performance Summary

On your dashboard you'll see a roll-up of your growth:

- **Per-domain averages** — your average score on each of the 6 Marshall domains, computed across all your **published and acknowledged** observations
- **Most-recent indicator ratings** — the latest score you got on each indicator
- **Observation counts** — how many Mini, Formal, and Annual Summary observations contributed

**Nothing in the summary comes from drafts.** If your principal scored something but hasn't published yet, it doesn't count.

## Exporting Your Records

**URL:** `/reports` (called *Exports* in your nav)

You can export your full record at any time — a fantastic resource for:
- HR conversations
- Portfolio-building
- Applying for new positions
- Personal review

### ① Who & when
- You (pre-selected — you can only export your own data)
- **From / To** — date range, default: the full year
- **Observation type** — Mini, Formal, Annual Summary (any combination)

### ② What to include
One-click presets:
- **Full observation** — everything
- **Scores only** — just rubric scores
- **Strengths only** — your Glows
- **Growth areas only** — your Grows + Next Steps
- **Feedback only** — all feedback, no scores
- **Teacher folder copy** — everything for your portfolio

Or tick individual checkboxes: summary, scores, glows, grows, focus areas, next steps, signatures.

### ③ Download
- **CSV** — opens in Excel / Google Sheets. 8 different row shapes to pick from.
- **PDF** — print-ready document. Open in new tab → **Print → Save as PDF** to save or email.

**Typical teacher use cases:**
- **End-of-year portfolio** → pick *Teacher folder copy* → PDF → save with the year in the filename
- **Prep for an evaluation meeting** → *Full observation* → PDF of the specific observation being discussed
- **Track your own growth** → *Scores only* → CSV → make a chart in Excel of your indicator scores over time
- **Celebrate with your PLC** → *Strengths only* → share your Glows with peers

You will never see other teachers in your exports — the system strictly scopes you to yourself.

## Privacy & Your Rights

**What is stored about you:**
- Your name, email, title, role, school
- Every observation (scripted notes, scores, evidence, feedback, signatures)
- Every focus area (active and closed)
- Your responses to observations
- Login timestamps

**What is NOT stored:**
- Your personal files / documents
- Classroom video or photos (the platform doesn't do media)
- Students' personally-identifying information (your principal is instructed to use initials only)
- Any health, financial, or protected personal data

**Who can see your data:**
| Person | Sees |
|---|---|
| **You** | Everything shown above except private appraiser notes |
| **Your assigned appraiser(s)** | Everything including their own private notes |
| **Your assigned coach(es)** | Everything EXCEPT private appraiser notes |
| **Your superintendent** | Published observations only — no drafts, no private notes |
| **Your super admin** | Everything (for support/troubleshooting; actions are logged) |
| **Other teachers** | Nothing — they can't see your data at all |

**Your rights:**
- **Request your full record** — use the Reports page to download CSV and PDF any time
- **Add responses forever** — every time you acknowledge, your response is permanent
- **Request corrections** — if you believe an observation contains a factual error, talk to your principal. They can issue a correcting observation (they cannot edit the original — signed records are immutable).
- **Know who's accessed your record** — ask your Super Admin for the activity log (every action is logged)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I was observed last week but I don't see anything in my account.**
Your principal probably hasn't published yet. Drafts are invisible to you. Give them a few days, or ask them directly.

**Q: I forgot my password.**
Ask your Super Admin (usually the district office) to reset it. Your password will be reset to `Alexander2026!` and you'll be forced to change it on your next login. All your other devices will be signed out automatically (for security).

**Q: I don't agree with a score. What do I do?**
1. When you acknowledge, use the **response** field to document your disagreement — specific, professional, evidence-based.
2. Talk to your principal in person.
3. If unresolved, follow your district's grievance / evaluation dispute process. Your association representative can help.
The score itself cannot be changed once published — that's by design, for legal reliability — but your response is a permanent part of the record.

**Q: My principal wrote something that seems unprofessional. Can I request it be removed?**
No — published observations are immutable. But your response is your chance to add your perspective, and if there's genuinely inappropriate content, escalate to your HR / association representative.

**Q: Why can't I see my principal's "private notes"?**
By design. Private notes are for the principal's own process (drafts, reminders, questions for post-conference). If it's important enough to share with you, they put it in the overall summary or a grow. This separation actually protects you — it means the principal can jot down questions without having to worry about how you'd interpret them.

**Q: Can I see how I'm trending?**
On your dashboard, the performance summary shows per-domain averages across all your observations. For a full trend chart, export CSV (*Scores only*) and chart it in Excel or Google Sheets.

**Q: What if I change schools mid-year?**
Your new principal will be added as an appraiser by the Super Admin. Your observation history comes with you — nothing is lost.

**Q: Can I see my coach's notes?**
No — coaches don't take notes in the platform (by design — keeps coaching confidential and separate from evaluation). If you want to remember something your coach said, write it down yourself.

**Q: What if I'm leaving the district?**
Export your full record as PDF before you leave — use the *Teacher folder copy* preset. Keep a copy for your own files; districts retain your record per state policy.
